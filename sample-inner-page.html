ZERO

  • Zero's origins can be traced back to Brahmagupta in 628, with the Babylonians and Olmecs using distinct symbols for the value. The Greeks integrated zero into their Sexagesimal system, while China used a blank space in their decimal counting rods system. The dot was first used in the Bakhshali manuscript.

    • The number 0 is fascinating - it represents an empty quantity, yet has a big impact in mathematics as the additive identity of many algebraic structures.
    • Zero is the first whole number in our system. It has revolutionized Mathematics and Science, making our current accounting and calculus possible. Without zero, our numbers wouldn't exist. Its impact on our lives and the world is truly remarkable.
    • India developed the concept of zero into a numeral and used mathematics in astronomy and philosophy.
  • Project Zero studies the development of zero in India, suggesting that cultural and philosophical factors may have been crucial to its mathematical concept.
  • The numeral zero was introduced by Brahmagupta in 628 using a dot underneath a number. He established standard rules for reaching zero through addition and subtraction.
  • Zero was discovered in India and the oldest recorded example dates back to the ninth century.
  • India may have invented the mathematical zero digit, according to Project Zero's secretary Gobets.
