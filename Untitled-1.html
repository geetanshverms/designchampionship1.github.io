YOGA

Yoga itself is more of a practice and philosophy rather than a collection of "discoveries" or "inventions." However, over time, different practitioners and philosophers have contributed to the evolution of yogic practices, techniques, and philosophies.

FATHER OF YOGA

Patanjali is the Father of Yoga, who explained its true purpose in his Yoga Sutras. Raja Yoga has been studied for thousands of years, and Veda Vyasa's commentary on the Sutras is an invaluable resource for practitioners.

  • Yoga combines spirituality and science for optimal health. It balances mind and body, and is the epitome of healthy living.
  • The word ‘Yoga’ is derived from the Sanskrit root ‘Yuj’, meaning ‘to join’ or ‘to yoke’ or ‘to unite’.
  • Yoga can unite individual consciousness with Universal Consciousness, creating harmony between mind, body, and nature. Science supports this concept, as everything is believed to be a manifestation of the same quantum firmament.
  • A yogi recognizes the interconnectedness of all things, achieved through attaining mukti, nirvana, or moksha.
  • Yoga aims to achieve self-realization and overcome suffering to attain pure joy and illumination, also known as Moksha or Kaivalya. Consistent practice can liberate us from ignorance and lead us to a state of freedom.
  • Living with freedom in all walks of life, health and harmony shall be the main objectives of Yoga practice.
  • Yoga is an inner science that empowers individuals to achieve union and take control of their lives. It unlocks potential and enables control of one's destiny.
  • Yoga is an ancient tradition dating back over 4,000 years. Its benefits cater to both physical and spiritual health, making it a valuable practice.
  • Basic humane values are the very identity of Yoga Sadhana.
