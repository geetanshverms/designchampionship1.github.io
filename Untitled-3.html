Ayurveda

  • Āyurveda is a Sanskrit term that is made up of two words - āyus and veda. The word āyus means "life" or "longevity", while veda means "knowledge". Together, the term Āyurveda translates to "knowledge of life and longevity" or "knowledge of longevity".

    • Ayurveda and Siddha are ancient systems of medicine in South Asia, widely used today to promote health and wellness. Ayurveda is known as "medicine" in rural areas, while Siddha medicine primarily uses herbs and minerals.
    • Ayurveda is an alternative medicine from India and Nepal, but it's not scientifically proven.
    • Ayurveda is a holistic system that promotes lifestyle changes and natural therapies to restore balance between the body, mind, spirit, and environment, leading to optimal well-being.
  • Ayurveda uses eight diagnostic methods, including pulse, urine, stool, tongue, speech, touch, vision, and appearance. Ayurvedic practitioners use their senses skillfully, such as hearing to observe breathing and speech. The study of lethal points, or marman marma, is important in Ayurvedic practice.
  • Most Ayurvedic remedies are plant-based, with about 90% of treatments coming from roots, leaves, fruits, bark, and seeds. Triphala is a commonly used herbal formula, and Ayurveda also uses Ashwagandha and Tulsi.
  • Ayurveda utilizes animal products, fats, and minerals in its medicine and healing practices. It's interesting to see how cultures approach healing differently.
